About EliS

The ELiS writing system is is composed of 95 visographemes, which are distributed in four groups, which form its basic structure: Handshape (CD), with 10 visographems; Palm Orientation (OP), with 6 visographemes; Location (PA), with 35 visographemes; and Movement (M), with 44 visographemes.

The ELiS visografemas are organized in a fixed structure, which serves for any type of sign: Handshape, Palm Orientation, Location and Movement. This is to say that, when a word is written using ELiS, first write the Finger Configuration (CD) visografemas, followed by, Palm Orientation (OP), Location (PA) and finally, Movement (M) being that some sign that do not have movement therefore it is left out when being written. The reader should first take note of the description of each visographeme and the movements they suggest, ultimatly, the reader will be able to perform the Libras sign for BONITO.

CD: thumb and other fingers extended

OP: palm facing backward

PA: hand in front of the face

M: closing the hand finger by finger (drumming of fingers without repetition)



In ELiS there are six types of signs: one-handed, two-handed symmetrical, two-handed asymmetrical, two-handed semi-symmetrical, with supporting hand and compound. The sign tgçQÄ (BONITO), presented previously, is an example of a one-handed sign, which is the most basic type of sign in ELiS. The other types of signs, despite following the same sequence of organization of visographemes, have specific requirements in their spelling.
